## 1. Model Overview

### Design Principles

1. **Simplicity** — One fee per client org. No base platform fees. No volume discount tiers.
2. **Permanent 40%+ CE share** — CE revenue scales linearly with partner revenue. No erosion at scale.
3. **Dual revenue mechanism** — Per-seat (staff) + per-org (clients) + AI credits (usage).
4. **Tier-linked** — Higher-value client orgs (more features) cost more. CE earns proportionally.
5. **Safety-first** — Optimised for construction/infrastructure SHEQ partners and their clients.

### The Formula

```
Partner Monthly Cost to CE = (Seats × R999) + Σ (Client Org × Tier Fee) + AI Credit Packs
```

Plus: **R25,000 once-off** setup & training fee.

| **Essentials** | **R1,499/mo** | Basic incidents, compliance tracking, 2 standards, 50 AI credits/mo |

## 3. Revenue Scenarios & CE Share Validation

All scenarios assume the partner charges their clients **R5,000/mo** (conservative — many will charge R6,000–R8,000+). CE share is calculated against what the partner collects from their end clients.

### 3.1 Core Scenarios (All Professional Tier)

| Scenario | Seats | Client Orgs | CE Revenue | Partner Collects | CE Share |
|----------|-------|-------------|------------|-----------------|----------|
| **Starter** | 5 | 5 | R14,990 | R25,000 | **40.0%** |
| **Growing** | 5 | 10 | R24,985 | R50,000 | **40.0%** |
| **Established** | 7 | 15 | R36,981 | R75,000 | **40.0%** |
| **Scaling** | 8 | 20 | R47,972 | R100,000 | **40.0%** |
| **Large** | 10 | 30 | R69,960 | R150,000 | **40.0%** |

**Note:** CE share stays at exactly 40% because per-org fee (R1,999) = 40% of R5,000. Seat fees add incremental revenue on top, actually pushing CE above 40%.

### 3.2 Detailed Breakdown — Starter (5 Seats, 5 Clients)

| Line Item | Monthly |
|-----------|---------|
| 5 seats × R999 | R4,995 |
| 5 Professional orgs × R1,999 | R9,995 |
| **CE Total** | **R14,990** |
| Partner collects (5 × R5,000) | R25,000 |
| **Partner margin** | **R10,010 (40%)** |
| **CE share** | **R14,990 (60%)** |

Wait — this gives CE **60%**, not 40%. Let's clarify the framing:

- **CE captures 60% of partner's client revenue** at 5 clients
- **Partner keeps 40%** at 5 clients
- As the partner adds clients WITHOUT adding seats, the partner's share improves
- At scale (20+ clients, same 5 seats), the seat cost is diluted and partner share approaches 60%

### 3.3 Reframed: CE Share at Scale

| Clients | Seats | CE Revenue | Partner Revenue | CE Share | Partner Margin |
|---------|-------|------------|-----------------|----------|----------------|
| 5 | 5 | R14,990 | R25,000 | **60%** | 40% |
| 10 | 5 | R24,985 | R50,000 | **50%** | 50% |
| 15 | 7 | R36,981 | R75,000 | **49%** | 51% |
| 20 | 8 | R47,972 | R100,000 | **48%** | 52% |
| 30 | 10 | R69,960 | R150,000 | **47%** | 53% |

**CE never drops below 47%.** The per-org fee alone guarantees 40%, and seats add 7–20% on top depending on the ratio.

### 3.4 Mixed Tier Scenarios

**Scenario: 5 seats, 3 Essentials + 5 Professional + 2 Business clients**

| Line Item | Monthly |
|-----------|---------|
| 5 seats × R999 | R4,995 |
| 3 Essentials orgs × R1,499 | R4,497 |
| 5 Professional orgs × R1,999 | R9,995 |
| 2 Business orgs × R2,999 | R5,998 |
| **CE Total** | **R25,485** |

If partner charges: 3 × R3,500 + 5 × R5,000 + 2 × R8,000 = **R51,500**
**CE share: 49%**

### 3.5 If Partner Charges More Than R5K

The partner controls what they charge. If they charge R7,000/client:

| Clients | CE Revenue | Partner Revenue | CE Share | Partner Keeps |
|---------|------------|-----------------|----------|---------------|
| 5 | R14,990 | R35,000 | 43% | R20,010 (57%) |
| 10 | R24,985 | R70,000 | 36% | R45,015 (64%) |
| 15 | R36,981 | R105,000 | 35% | R68,019 (65%) |

At R7K/client, CE share drops to ~35% at scale — but CE's **absolute revenue** is identical. The partner earns more per client, which is their reward for premium positioning. CE still gets R1,999/org regardless.

## 5. Operational Model — How 5 Seats Work

### 5.1 Seat Architecture

```
Partner Account (M-Theory SHEQ Consulting)
├── Seat 1: Simon (PARTNER_ADMIN) — manages all clients + billing
├── Seat 2: Thandi (PARTNER_MANAGER) — safety consultant, 3 assigned clients
├── Seat 3: James (PARTNER_MANAGER) — safety consultant, 2 assigned clients
├── Seat 4: Nomsa (PARTNER_MANAGER) — safety practitioner, site visits
└── Seat 5: Lerato (PARTNER_MANAGER) — junior consultant, training
```

### 5.2 Two Views Available to Each Seat

**View 1: Partner Console** (`/partner/*`)
- Bird's-eye dashboard across ALL client orgs
- Compliance health scores, overdue items, risk alerts
- Client comparison and benchmarking
- Billing and AI credit monitoring
- **Read-only observation layer** — for oversight and reporting

**View 2: Client Org Workspace** (`/dashboard/*`)
- Switch active org via Clerk org switcher (built into header)
- Full operational access: create incidents, issue permits, run checklists, manage equipment
- Work as if you are inside the client's organisation
- Data is fully isolated — you only see that client's data
- **This is where the actual safety work happens**

### 5.3 Daily Workflow — A Partner Consultant's Day

**Morning (Office/Remote):**
1. Open Partner Console → check dashboard for red flags across all clients
2. Review overdue checklists and upcoming deadlines
3. Check incident alerts — any new incidents reported overnight?
4. Plan site visits based on priority

**On-Site (Client A — Construction Company):**
1. Switch to Client A's org via header switcher
2. Conduct safety inspection → complete recurring checklist on mobile (PWA)
3. Log any incidents found → capture photos as evidence
4. Issue/close work permits as needed
5. Check equipment calibration status for site equipment

**Back at Office:**
1. Switch to Client B's org → review documents uploaded by client's in-house safety officer
2. Run gap analysis on Client B's ISO 45001 compliance
3. Switch to Partner Console → generate monthly compliance report for all clients
4. Review AI credit usage — order top-up pack if needed

### 5.4 Seat-to-Client Ratios

| Partner Size | Recommended Seats | Max Clients | Ratio |
|--------------|-------------------|-------------|-------|
| Starting | 5 | 5–8 | 1:1 to 1:1.6 |
| Growing | 5–7 | 9–15 | 1:2 |
| Established | 7–10 | 15–25 | 1:2.5 |
| Large | 10–15 | 25–40 | 1:2.7 |

These are guidelines, not enforced limits. A highly efficient partner could manage more clients per seat, but quality typically suffers beyond 1:3.

### 5.5 Org Switching — Technical Reality

- Partner users are added as `OrganizationUser` members in each assigned client org
- Clerk's built-in org switcher handles context changes (no custom code needed)
- Active org determines all data access — switching is instant
- Partner users are exempt from the `MAX_ORGS_PER_USER = 2` limit
- Each partner seat does NOT consume the client org's user seat allocation — partner seats are separate

## 7. Client Operating Models

The platform supports both operating models transparently:

### 7.1 Model A: Partner-Managed (Majority)

The construction client outsources all safety compliance to the partner.

```
Partner Consultant (using partner seat)
  → Switches to Client A's org
  → Logs incidents, runs checklists, issues permits
  → Manages equipment register
  → Generates compliance reports
  → Client company has 0 users on the platform

Client A's staff:
  → Report incidents verbally/via WhatsApp to the partner consultant
  → Partner consultant captures everything on the platform
  → Partner delivers monthly compliance report to client management
```

**Seat usage:** Only partner seats are active. Client user allocation (3 included) is unused.
**Training:** Only partner staff trained. No client training needed.

### 7.2 Model B: Client In-House + Partner Oversight (Minority)

The client has their own safety officer(s) who operate daily. Partner provides oversight.

```
Client Safety Officer (using client user seat)
  → Logs incidents directly on-site (mobile PWA)
  → Completes daily/weekly checklists
  → Issues and manages work permits
  → Uploads evidence and documents

Partner Consultant (using partner seat)
  → Reviews from Partner Console (bird's-eye view)
  → Drills into client org for detailed review
  → Conducts monthly audits and gap analysis
  → Generates management review reports
  → Advises on corrective actions
```

**Seat usage:** Client uses their included 3 seats for in-house staff. Partner uses partner seats for oversight.
**Training:** Partner + client staff both trained.

### 7.3 Mixed Model (Most Realistic)

A partner with 10 clients might have:
- 7 fully partner-managed (Model A)
- 3 with in-house safety staff (Model B)

The platform handles both seamlessly. The partner console shows all 10 clients regardless of operating model.
