## 7. CIDB Register Prospecting Guide

### What Is It?
The Construction Industry Development Board (CIDB) maintains a public register of every graded contractor in South Africa at **registers.cidb.org.za**. All public sector clients are legally required to use CIDB-registered contractors.

### Why Grade 5-9?
CIDB grades contractors 1-9 based on capability (financial and track record). Higher grades = larger contracts = more compliance requirements:

| Grade | Contract Value Range | Profile |
|---|---|---|
| 1-4 | Up to R13M | Small contractors, limited compliance needs |
| **5** | Up to R40M | Mid-size, starting to need formal SHEQ systems |
| **6** | Up to R130M | Established firms, ISO compliance often required for tenders |
| **7** | Up to R200M | Large contractors, multiple ISO standards expected |
| **8** | Up to R500M | Major contractors, comprehensive QMS mandatory |
| **9** | Unlimited | Tier 1 contractors (WBHO, Murray & Roberts, etc.) |

### How to Prospect
1. Go to registers.cidb.org.za
2. Search by province, grading level, and class of works (e.g., CE = Civil Engineering, GB = General Building)
3. Export or note company names, contact details
4. Cross-reference with LinkedIn to find SHEQ managers or directors
5. Reach out with targeted messaging about how ConformEdge helps win tenders through organized compliance documentation

### Target Sweet Spot
- **Grade 5-7** are the sweet spot — big enough to need compliance tools, small enough to not have enterprise systems (IsoMetrix). These map to ConformEdge's Professional and Business tiers (R5,499-R8,499/mo).
- **Grade 8-9** are Enterprise tier prospects (R16,999+/mo) but have longer sales cycles.
