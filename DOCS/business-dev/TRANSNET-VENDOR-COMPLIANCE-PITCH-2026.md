## Executive Summary

Transnet manages thousands of vendors, contractors, and service providers across six operating divisions — each with different compliance requirements spanning the OHS Act, ISO standards, BBBEE, COIDA, RSR regulations, and environmental legislation.

Today, contractor compliance is tracked through fragmented manual processes — spreadsheets, email chains, physical document submission, and periodic audits. This creates blind spots: expired certifications go unnoticed, Section 37(2) agreements lapse, COIDA Letters of Good Standing aren't verified, and incident reporting is inconsistent across projects.

**ConformEdge is an AI-powered SHEQ & Compliance Management platform** purpose-built for South African regulatory requirements. It provides a single system to onboard, track, verify, and audit contractor compliance — across all divisions, all projects, all standards.

This document outlines how ConformEdge addresses Transnet's specific contractor compliance challenges and the value it delivers at each layer of the organisation.

## The Problem — Fragmented Contractor Compliance

### What Transnet Faces Today

| Challenge | Impact |
|---|---|
| **Thousands of contractors across 6 divisions** | No unified view of who is compliant and who isn't |
| **Section 37(2) agreements managed manually** | Expired agreements expose Transnet to OHS Act liability |
| **COIDA Letters of Good Standing not tracked** | Transnet can be held liable for unregistered contractor claims |
| **BBBEE certificates expire without notice** | Invalid BBBEE scoring on active contracts |
| **ISO certifications verified at tender stage only** | Certifications expire mid-contract with no alert |
| **Incident reporting varies by division** | Inconsistent data makes LTIFR trending unreliable |
| **Environmental compliance (CEMP) is project-specific** | No cross-project visibility on environmental permits and obligations |
| **RSR Safety Permit compliance** | Contractor alignment with Transnet's Safety Management System is audited but tracked offline |
| **LTIFR rose to 0.77 in 2024/25** | Strengthened safety controls require better tracking tools |

### The Cost of the Status Quo

- **Regulatory exposure:** Transnet remains liable under OHS Act Section 37(1) if Section 37(2) agreements are not current
- **Audit failures:** Auditor-General findings on irregular expenditure and governance gaps compound with poor contractor documentation
- **Safety incidents:** 1,833 safety-related negative events in 2024/25 — each one requires documented investigation, root cause analysis, and corrective action
- **R1.59 billion** in revenue losses from security and infrastructure incidents — compliance documentation is critical for insurance and recovery claims
- **Procurement delays:** Manual verification of BBBEE, COIDA, ISO, and CIDB certificates slows contractor onboarding

## Transnet-Specific Use Cases

### Use Case 1: Construction Contractor Pre-Qualification

**Scenario:** Transnet Engineering issues a tender for locomotive maintenance. 40 contractors respond.

**Today:** Procurement manually verifies BBBEE certificates, COIDA letters, ISO certifications, CIDB grading, and tax compliance certificates for each bidder. Documents arrive via email in different formats. Verification takes 2-3 weeks.

**With ConformEdge:** Contractors upload all compliance documents to the platform before bidding. The procurement team runs a compliance report showing each contractor's certification status, expiry dates, and compliance score — in minutes. Non-compliant bidders are flagged automatically.

### Use Case 2: Port Terminal Contractor Safety Monitoring

**Scenario:** Transnet Port Terminals manages 15 active contractors at Durban Container Terminal. Three incidents occur in a month.

**Today:** Each incident is reported via paper forms. Investigation happens via email. COIDA W.Cl.2 submission is manual. LTIFR is calculated quarterly from aggregated spreadsheets.

**With ConformEdge:** Incidents are logged on-site via mobile phone — photos, witness statements, location data captured immediately. Investigation uses fishbone analysis with 5-whys. W.Cl.2 is auto-generated. LTIFR dashboard updates in real-time. Trend analysis flags the contractor with the rising incident rate.

### Use Case 3: Cross-Divisional Compliance Audit

**Scenario:** Auditor-General requests compliance evidence for 50 contractors across TFR, TPT, and TE.

**Today:** Each division compiles its own documentation. Formats differ. Some documents are missing. Compilation takes 4-6 weeks.

**With ConformEdge:** Admin generates audit packs for all 50 contractors from the Enterprise dashboard. Each pack includes: current certifications, Section 37(2) agreement status, incident history, training records, and compliance scores. Delivered as branded PDFs — in hours.
