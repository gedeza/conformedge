## 11. Presentation Strategy

### Tone
- **Confident but respectful** — The platform is built, deployed, and proven (unlike the original pitch which was conceptual)
- **Construction-fluent** — Use their language: site inductions, PTW, toolbox talks, welding certs, OHS file
- **ROI-focused** — Lead with business impact, not features

### Key Messages
1. "This is not a concept. This is deployed and running." (Directly addressing the gap from Vela's original pitch)
2. "One platform for 9001 + 14001 + 45001 — one document can evidence multiple clauses"
3. "Your field teams get a mobile app that works offline on construction sites"
4. "AI classifies a 24-page safety management plan in 3 seconds"
5. "One click generates your complete audit pack — no more week-long scrambles"

### Things to Avoid
- Do not reference the PRASA tender controversy — frame around "regulatory scrutiny in the current environment" if compliance urgency comes up
- Do not oversell features that aren't built (everything in the demo is real)
- Do not compare directly to their current tools — ask what they use first
