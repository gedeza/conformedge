## 1. STATE-OWNED ENTERPRISES (SOEs)

### 1.1 Eskom Holdings SOC Ltd
- **Sector:** Energy/Power generation
- **ISO Standards:** ISO 9001 (HR division certified via NQA Africa), ISO 14001 (multiple divisions/power stations certified), ISO 45001 (actively pursuing)
- **Key Facts:**
  - Eskom issued a tender (Oct 2023) for ISO 9001, ISO 14001, and ISO 45001 auditing and certification services across the entire group
  - Matimba Power Station has undergone ISO 14001 EMS audits
  - SHEQ policy drives environmental and safety management across all power stations
  - Eskom Uganda subsidiary holds triple certification (9001, 14001, 45001)
- **Relevant ConformEdge Standards:** ISO 9001, ISO 14001, ISO 45001, ISO 55001 (asset management — attempted at Kusile/Medupi), ISO 31000 (risk management)
- **Why ConformEdge:** Eskom has fragmented ISO certification across divisions. A unified platform could consolidate SHEQ compliance, track multi-standard certification across 30+ power stations, and manage the transition to integrated management systems. The 2023 tender for certification services signals active investment in compliance infrastructure.
- **Procurement:** Direct tender via Eskom procurement portal + CSD
- **Estimated Value:** R400K–R600K/year (enterprise tier, multi-division)

### 1.2 Transnet SOC Ltd
- **Sector:** Transport/Logistics (rail, ports, pipelines)
- **Divisions:** Transnet Freight Rail, Transnet National Ports Authority (TNPA), Transnet Port Terminals, Transnet Pipelines, Transnet Engineering
- **ISO Standards:** ISO 31000 (risk management, referenced in Integrated Assurance Plan), ISO 14001 (ports environmental management), ISO 9001 (engineering/manufacturing divisions), ISO 55001 (asset management — critical for rail/port infrastructure)
- **Key Facts:**
  - TNPA manages 8 major seaports with individual Environmental Management Systems
  - Transnet's Integrated Management System referenced in contractor management procedures
  - Annual report references ISO 31000-aligned risk management
- **Relevant ConformEdge Standards:** ISO 9001, ISO 14001, ISO 45001, ISO 55001, ISO 31000, ISO 28000 (supply chain security — ports)
- **Why ConformEdge:** Multiple operating divisions each managing separate compliance. IMS engine would consolidate cross-standard requirements. Port-specific environmental compliance tracking would be high value.
- **Procurement:** Transnet tender bulletin + CSD
- **Estimated Value:** R400K–R600K/year (enterprise, multi-division)

### 1.3 Passenger Rail Agency of South Africa (PRASA)
- **Sector:** Rail transport
- **ISO Standards:** Safety Management System (mandated by Railway Safety Regulator), ISO 45001 (OHS — applicable), ISO 55001 (asset management — previously attempted)
- **Key Facts:**
  - Railway Safety Regulator (RSR) mandates documented Safety Management System for operating permits
  - PRASA obtained 3-year RSR safety permit (valid through Aug 2025), closed 144 of 200 improvement directives
  - ISO 55000 asset management implementation was commissioned but cancelled
  - PRASA's SHE Specification references OHSAS/ISO 45001 requirements for contractors
- **Relevant ConformEdge Standards:** ISO 45001, ISO 55001, ISO 9001, ISO 31000
- **Why ConformEdge:** PRASA's maturing safety management systems need digital tracking. Closing improvement directives and maintaining RSR compliance is exactly what ConformEdge's CAPA and assessment tracking handles. Asset management gap is a major opportunity.
- **Procurement:** PRASA SCM (Supply Chain Management) + CSD
- **Estimated Value:** R300K–R500K/year

### 1.4 South African National Roads Agency Ltd (SANRAL)
- **Sector:** Road infrastructure
- **ISO Standards:** ISO 9001 (quality management for road construction oversight), ISO 14001 (environmental management for road projects), ISO 45001 (construction safety), ISO 55001 (road asset management)
- **Why ConformEdge:** Road infrastructure asset management and environmental compliance for major construction projects. SANRAL oversees billions in road contracts requiring contractor compliance verification.
- **Procurement:** SANRAL tender portal + CSD
- **Estimated Value:** R250K–R400K/year

### 1.5 Airports Company South Africa (ACSA)
- **Sector:** Aviation/Airport management
- **Manages:** 9 airports including OR Tambo, Cape Town International, King Shaka
- **ISO Standards:** ISO 9001 (service quality), ISO 14001 (environmental), ISO 45001 (safety), ISO 55001 (airport infrastructure asset management)
- **Key Facts:**
  - Won 2025 Airport Service Quality (ASQ) awards — evidence of quality focus
  - 94.6% state-owned
  - References ISO in governance documentation
- **Why ConformEdge:** Airport operations require integrated quality, safety, and environmental management across 9 facilities. Multi-site compliance tracking is a core ConformEdge strength.
- **Procurement:** ACSA procurement + CSD
- **Estimated Value:** R300K–R500K/year

### 1.6 Denel SOC Ltd
- **Sector:** Defence/Aerospace manufacturing
- **ISO Standards:** ISO 9001 (manufacturing quality — critical for defence), AS9100 (aerospace quality), ISO 14001, ISO 45001, ISO 27001 (information security — defence sensitive)
- **Why ConformEdge:** Defence manufacturing requires rigorous quality management and document control. Despite financial difficulties, Denel divisions maintaining international contracts must hold ISO certifications.
- **Procurement:** Denel SCM + CSD (note: Denel under financial restructuring)

## 4. GOVERNMENT PROCUREMENT PROCESS

### 4.1 How Government Buys SaaS

**Step 1: CSD Registration (Mandatory)**
- Register at secure.csd.gov.za (free)
- Verification: CIPC, SARS, Labour, Home Affairs
- Required for ALL government business

**Step 2: B-BBEE Compliance**
- EME (turnover ≤R10M): B-BBEE affidavit sufficient
- QSE (R10M–R50M): May need SANAS-accredited verification
- Scoring: 80/20 or 90/10 price-preference (PPPFA)
- Higher B-BBEE level = better scoring in tenders

**Step 3: Tender Documentation**
- SBD 1 (Invitation to Bid)
- SBD 4 (Declaration of Interest)
- SBD 6.1 (Preference Points Claim)
- SBD 8 (Declaration of Bidder's Past SCM Practices)
- SBD 9 (Certificate of Independent Bid Determination)
- Tax Compliance Pin (SARS)
- Company registration (CIPC)
- Bank confirmation letter

**Step 4: Procurement Channels**

| Channel | When to Use | Threshold |
|---|---|---|
| **Request for Quotation (RFQ)** | Below R1M | 3+ quotes required |
| **Competitive Bid (Tender)** | Above R1M | Formal tender process |
| **SITA Transversal Contract** | National/Provincial ICT | Must go through SITA for cloud/SaaS |
| **Direct SOE Procurement** | SOE-specific needs | Via SOE tender bulletin |
| **Municipal SCM** | Municipal purchases | Via municipality SCM unit |

### 4.2 SITA Considerations

- **National/Provincial departments MUST procure cloud/SaaS through SITA**
- SITA certifies all ICT services for government compliance
- Transversal contracts are preferred (blanket agreements government-wide)
- SITA is actively establishing new transversal contracts for cloud services
- Getting on a SITA transversal contract = access to all national/provincial departments

### 4.3 Municipal Procurement (Separate from SITA)

- Municipalities are NOT required to go through SITA
- They follow MFMA procurement rules independently
- Each municipality has its own SCM (Supply Chain Management) unit
- Published on etenders.gov.za or municipal websites
- Lower barriers to entry than SITA channel

### 4.4 SOE Procurement (Separate from SITA)

- SOEs have independent procurement functions
- Follow PFMA requirements
- Publish tenders on own portals and etenders.gov.za
- Can procure SaaS directly without SITA involvement

## 5. STRATEGIC RECOMMENDATIONS

### 5.1 Priority Targets (Quick Wins)

1. **Water Boards** — 9 entities, similar needs, manageable size, environmental + quality compliance. Target Umgeni Water (KZN, existing PRASA connection in demo data) and Rand Water (largest) first.

2. **PRASA** — Already have PRASA safety plan PDF in demo seed data. Safety management system compliance is regulatory-mandated. High urgency given RSR permit conditions.

3. **Municipalities (Metros)** — Start with better-governed metros (Cape Town, eThekwini). Municipal procurement is simpler than SITA channel. ISO alignment helps AG audit outcomes.

### 5.2 Medium-Term Targets

4. **Eskom** — Massive multi-division opportunity but complex procurement. Start with a single division/power station as proof of concept.

5. **Transnet (TNPA)** — Port environmental management is a focused entry point.

6. **ACSA** — 9 airports, service quality focus, state-owned but commercially minded.

### 5.3 Long-Term / Strategic

7. **SITA Transversal Contract** — Getting listed enables access to ALL national/provincial departments. Long process (12-18 months) but highest leverage.

8. **DMRE** — As regulator, if DMRE adopts ConformEdge it creates downstream demand from every mining company needing to demonstrate MHSA compliance.

## 6. COMPETITIVE ADVANTAGES FOR GOVERNMENT

1. **SA-Built, SA-Hosted** — POPIA compliant, data sovereignty, local support
2. **B-BBEE Points** — ISU Tech's B-BBEE level improves tender scoring
3. **Multi-Standard IMS** — Government entities typically need 3-5 standards simultaneously
4. **DMRE/MHSA Module** — Unique SA-specific regulatory compliance (mining sector)
5. **POPIA Compliance** — Built-in, not bolted-on
6. **ECSA/SACPCMP** — Professional registration compliance for engineering departments
7. **AI-Powered Gap Analysis** — Reduces consultant dependency (significant cost savings)
8. **Audit Trail** — Critical for Auditor-General compliance
